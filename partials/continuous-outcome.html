Continuous Outcome

Things to consider

  • A study of the difference of means between experimental group and standard group
  • Large sample normal inference with equal underlying variance will be used to calculate the confidence interval at the analysis stage

Things to enter

  • One-sided significance level: The probability of choosing the alternative hypothesis when the null hypothesis is true (bad decision)
  • Target power: The probability of choosing the alternative hypothesis when the alternative hypothesis is true (good decision)
  • Allocation ratio: (number of subjects in experimental group)/(number of subjects in standard group)
  • Assumed mean in experimental group
  • Assumed mean in standard group
  • Assumed standard deviation
  • Non-inferiority margin: The largest difference in success rate that is clinically acceptable

Reference

M. D. Rothmann, B. L. Wiens, and I. S. F. Chan, Design and Analysis of Non-Inferiority Trials. CRC Press, 2008, p. 331-333
