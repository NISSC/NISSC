About

The Non-Inferiority Sample Size Calculator (NISSC) is a free web-based sample size calculator not only for non-inferiority studies but also for superiority trials with continuous, binary, and survival outcomes. It is an open-source web application available on GitHub and does not require downloads or installations.

A non-inferiority design is considered when we desire to show that the experimental treatment is shown to have similar effect, yet is less costly, better in terms of safety, and/or more convenient to administer than the active control.

The general hypotheses are

H0: The experimental treatment is inferior to the standard treatment by more than a pre-determined margin.

Ha: The experimental treatment is either better than the standard treatment or the experimental treatment is inferior to the standard treatment by less than a pre-determined margin.

M. D. Rothmann, B. L. Wiens, and I. S. F. Chan, Design and Analysis of Non-Inferiority Trials. CRC Press, 2008, p. 480

The non-inferiority margin is the smallest difference that is clinically meaningful or the largest difference that is clinically meaningless.

Good references are "Through the looking glass: understanding non-inferiority" by Schumi and Wittes and "Reporting of Noninferiority and Equivalence Randomized Trials: Extension of the CONSORT 2010 Statement" by Piaggio et al.
