Survival Outcome

Things to consider

  • A study to compare the hazard ratio between experimental group and standard group
  • Requires proportional hazard assumption and non-informative censoring
  • Large sample normal inference with unrestricted estimate of standard error will be used to calculate the confidence interval at the analysis stage

Things to enter

  • One-sided significance level: The probability of choosing the alternative hypothesis when the null hypothesis is true (bad decision)
  • Target power: The probability of choosing the alternative hypothesis when the alternative hypothesis is true (good decision)
  • Proportion of experimental group in total sample size
  • Probability of observing an event in experimental group: the value should be between 0 and 1
  • Probability of observing an event in standard group: the value should be between 0 and 1
  • Non-inferiority margin: the largest difference in the difference of means that is clinically acceptable

Reference

S.C. Chow, H. Wang, and J. Shao, Sample Size Calculations in Clinical Research, Second Edition. CRC Press, 2007, p. 174-179
