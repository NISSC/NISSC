Binary Outcome

Things to consider

  • Study the difference in proportions of success between experimental group and standard group
  • Wald's confidence interval without correction using unrestricted estimate of the standard error will be calculated at the analysis stage

Things to enter

  • One-sided significance level: The probability of choosing the alternative hypothesis when the null hypothesis is true (bad decision)
  • Target power: The probability of choosing the alternative hypothesis when the alternative hypothesis is true (good decision)
  • Allocation ratio (number of subjects in experimental group)/(number of subjects in standard group)
  • Assumed event rate in experimental group: The value should be between 0 and 1
  • Assumed event rate in standard group: The value should be between 0 and 1
  • Non-inferiority margin: The largest difference in the difference of means that is clinically acceptable

Reference

M. D. Rothmann, B. L. Wiens, and I. S. F. Chan, Design and Analysis of Non-Inferiority Trials. CRC Press, 2008, p. 268-271
