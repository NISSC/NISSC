Failing to reject the null hypothesis is not sufficient to say two treatments are identical.

Blackwelder, W. C. (1982). "Proving the null hypothesis in clinical trials."

General Hypotheses

H0: The experimental treatment is inferior to the standard treatment by more than a pre-determined margin.

Ha: The experimental treatment is either better than the standard treatment or the experimental treatment is inferior to the standard treatment by less than a pre-determined margin.

M. D. Rothmann, B. L. Wiens, and I. S. F. Chan, Design and Analysis of Non-Inferiority Trials. CRC Press, 2008, p. 480
